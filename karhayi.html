Chicken Karahi

Description

Chicken Karahi is one of the most beloved and iconic dishes of Pakistani cuisine, named after the heavy, deep-sided wok-like pan in which it is traditionally cooked. Known for its spicy, tangy, and robust flavor profile, it is distinct from other curries because it traditionally does not use onions, relying instead on a base of tomatoes, ginger, and garlic.

This specific recipe focuses on the "Lahori Style" Chicken Karahi, characterized by its thick masala, succulent pieces of bone-in chicken, and a vibrant garnish of fresh ginger julienne and green chilies. It is best enjoyed piping hot with fresh tandoori naan or khamiri roti.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into small karahi-style pieces
  • 5-6 large ripe tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1/2 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 1 teaspoon freshly crushed black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (roasted and crushed)
  • Fresh ginger (julienne cut) and cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. Searing the Chicken: Heat the oil in a karahi or deep wok over high heat. Add the chicken and fry until it changes color and develops a slight golden crust.
  2. Aromatics: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Softening Tomatoes: Place the halved tomatoes skin-side up over the chicken. Cover and cook on medium heat for 5-8 minutes. Once soft, peel off the tomato skins with tongs and discard.
  4. Developing the Masala: Mash the tomatoes into the chicken and increase the heat. Add the coriander powder, cumin, and salt. Stir-fry (bhunno) vigorously until the water evaporates and the oil begins to separate from the sauce.
  5. Finishing Touches: Lower the heat and stir in the yogurt and crushed black pepper. Add the green chilies. Simmer for 5 minutes until the gravy is thick and clings to the chicken.
  6. Garnish: Turn off the heat and top with a generous amount of fresh ginger julienne and chopped cilantro. Cover for 2 minutes before serving to let the aromas meld.
