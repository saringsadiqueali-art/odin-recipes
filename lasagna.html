Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a quintessential Italian comfort food consisting of stacked layers of wide pasta noodles alternated with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. It is a labor of love that fills the kitchen with a rich, savory aroma, making it a favorite for family gatherings and holiday dinners.

This specific recipe focuses on the "Lasagna al Forno" style, featuring a hearty meat sauce and a creamy cheese blend. The secret to a great lasagna lies in the balance of the acidity from the tomatoes, the creaminess of the cheese, and the tender texture of the pasta sheets.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara or pasta sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked and drained
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Steps

  1. Prep the Meat: In a large skillet, brown the ground beef with minced garlic over medium heat. Drain the excess fat and stir in the pasta sauce and oregano. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Mix the Cheese: In a small bowl, combine the ricotta cheese with the beaten egg and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Layering: Spread 1 cup of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Arrange 3 or 4 noodles over the sauce.
  4. Repeat: Spread a portion of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of mozzarella and more meat sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a thick layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  5. Bake: Cover the dish with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  6. Rest: Let the lasagna rest for 10 to 15 minutes before slicing to ensure the layers stay intact.
