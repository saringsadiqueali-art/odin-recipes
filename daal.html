Tarka Daal (Yellow Lentils)

Description

Daal is the heartbeat of every Pakistani household, serving as a staple comfort food that is both nutritious and deeply satisfying. While there are many varieties, "Tarka Daal" (usually made with split yellow peas or mung beans) is the most popular, prized for its creamy consistency and the aromatic infusion of spices.

The soul of this dish is the "Tarka" (tempering)—a technique where whole spices, garlic, and onions are fried in hot oil or ghee and poured over the cooked lentils just before serving. This creates a sudden sizzle and an explosion of flavor that defines the authentic Pakistani lentil experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Split Yellow Lentils (Moong or Masoor Daal), soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced (for Tarka)
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced (for Tarka)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried whole red chilies
  • 2 tablespoons Ghee or Butter
  • Fresh cilantro and green chilies for garnish

Steps

  1. Boil the Lentils: In a medium pot, combine the soaked lentils, 3 cups of water, chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Bring to a boil, then simmer on low heat until the lentils are completely soft and mushy.
  2. Whisking: Use a whisk or a wooden spoon to lightly mash the lentils until the consistency is smooth and creamy. Adjust the water if it becomes too thick.
  3. Prepare the Tarka: In a small frying pan, heat the ghee or oil. Add the sliced onions and fry until they start to turn golden brown.
  4. Sizzling Aromatics: Add the sliced garlic, cumin seeds, and dried red chilies to the frying pan. Continue frying until the garlic is golden and the cumin is fragrant (be careful not to burn it).
  5. The Tempering: Carefully pour the hot Tarka mixture directly into the pot of cooked lentils. It will sizzle loudly—quickly cover the pot with a lid for 2 minutes to trap the aromatic smoke inside.
  6. Serving: Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro and green chilies. Serve hot with steamed basmati rice (Daal Chawal) or fresh rotis.
